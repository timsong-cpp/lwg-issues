1507. promise::XXX_at_thread_exit functions have no synchronization requirements

Discussion:

Addresses US-199

promise::XXX_at_thread_exit functions have no synchronization requirements. Specifying synchronization for these member functions requires coordinating with the words in 31.6.6 [futures.promise]/21 and 25, which give synchronization requirements for promise::set_value and promise::set_exception (31.6.6 [futures.promise] p. 26 ff., p. 29 ff.).

[ Resolution proposed by ballot comment: ]

Change 31.6.6 [futures.promise]/21 to mention set_value_at_thread_exit and set_exception_at_thread_exit; with this text, replace 31.6.6 [futures.promise]/25 and add two new paragraphs, after 31.6.6 [futures.promise]/28 and 31.6.6 [futures.promise]/31.

[2011-03-8: Lawrence comments and drafts wording]

This comment applies as well to other *_at_thread_exit functions. The following resolution adds synchronization paragraphs to all of them and edits a couple of related synchronization paragraphs.

[2011-03-09: Hans and Anthony add some improvements]

[2011-03-19: Detlef comments]

In regard to the suggested part:

These operations do not provide any ordering guarantees with respect to other operations, except through operations on futures that reference the same shared state.

I would like this to change to:

These operations do not provide any ordering guarantees with respect to other operations on the same promise object. [Note: They synchronize with calls to operations on objects that refer to the same shared state according to 31.6.5 [futures.state]. — end note]

The current proposed resolution has exactly the same paragraph at for places. I propose to have it only once as new paragraph 2.

This also covers 1504 (US-196) and 1505 (US-197). US-197 is essentially rejected with this resolution, but a clarification is added that the normative wording is already in 31.6.5 [futures.state].
